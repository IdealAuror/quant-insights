Background

Paper: Nathan, Suominen & Tasa (2026), The Intramonth Momentum Cycle, SSRN

The paper finds that 77% of US momentum profits concentrate in 6 fixed trading days each month (days T-9 to T-4 before month-end, the PreTOM window). The mechanism: institutional month-end cash constraints force settlement selling, targeting dispensable loser stocks.

This study tests whether the same pattern holds in China's A-share market.

3. T+1 Rebound Is a Market-Wide Event, Not Loser-Specific

T+1: losers +47 bps (t=3.26), winners +56 bps (t=3.45). Winners rebound more, indicating T+1 is a market-level month-end effect (beta), not a loser-specific reversal (alpha).

T+2 full market giveback: losers -60 bps, winners -71 bps.

A-Share Intramonth Clock

T-6 / T-5 — Losers Dumped
Mutual fund month-end cash constraints drive targeted selling of losers. -75 bps in 2 days; winners hit but less severe.
T-4 ~ T-2 — Market Recovery
Selling pressure fades. Both losers and winners post positive returns.
T-1 — Last Trading Day
Market slightly weak, setting up for T+1 bounce.
T+1 — Broad Market Rebound
Losers +47 bps, winners +56 bps. Market-level beta effect, not loser-specific.
T+2 — Rebound Giveback
T+1 gains fully reversed: losers -60 bps, winners -71 bps.

US vs A-Share Comparison

US (Paper Findings)

  • PreTOM contributes 77% of momentum returns
  • Losers underperform -9.5 bps/day
  • Selling pressure gradual from T-9 to T-4
  • Mechanism: T+2 settlement + month-end redemptions
  • Crashes occur at month-start, not in PreTOM

A-Shares (This Study)

  • PreTOM contributes 70% of momentum returns
  • Losers underperform -9.9 bps/day
  • Selling pressure pulse-shaped on T-6/T-5
  • Mechanism: T+1 settlement + mutual fund redemptions
  • T+1 rebound then T+2 full giveback

Key difference: A-share T+1 settlement narrows the selling window vs US (originally T+2), producing a pulse instead of a gradual build-up. But direction and magnitude are highly consistent — loser-driven, no winner anomaly.

Conclusions

  1. A-shares exhibit the intramonth momentum cycle, consistent with the US paper. PreTOM contributes 70% of long-short momentum returns.
  2. Entirely loser-driven. Winners show no PreTOM anomaly; WML variation comes from targeted loser selling.
  3. Pulse-shaped selling concentrated on T-6 and T-5, not gradual as in the US. Linked to A-share T+1 settlement.
  4. T+1 rebound is beta, not alpha. Winners rebound more than losers — a market-wide month-end effect.
  5. Practical implication: Avoid holding losers on days T-6/T-5; potential short-term rebound opportunity after PreTOM (from T-3 onward).
